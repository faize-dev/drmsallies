MAXILLOFACIAL AND ORAL SURGEON BASED IN CAPE TOWN


Specialising in the surgical management of conditions affecting the mouth, jaws, face and neck, Dr Sallies has advanced training in Maxillofacial and Oral Surgery and offers a comprehensive range of procedures.

These include wisdom tooth removal, dental implant placement, corrective jaw surgery, facial trauma management and reconstructive surgery.

Procedures

Procedures

We offer the following procedures:


Wisdom Teeth Removal

Wisdom teeth removal is a surgical procedure to extract one or more third molars, often to prevent pain, infection, or crowding of other teeth.

Dental Implants

Dental implants are artificial tooth roots placed into the jawbone to support replacement teeth, restoring function and aesthetics after tooth loss.

Facial Trauma

Treatment of facial trauma involves repairing fractures and soft tissue damage to restore function and appearance after trauma.

Orthognathic Surgery

Orthognathic surgery corrects jaw misalignment and facial imbalances by repositioning the jaws, improving both function and appearance.


TMJ Disorder

Treatment for TMJ disorder addresses pain and dysfunction in the jaw joint, using therapies ranging from conservative management to surgical intervention.

Oral Pathology

Oral pathology involves the diagnosis and treatment of diseases affecting the mouth, jaws, and related structures, including biopsies and lesion management.

Aesthetic Procedures

Aesthetic procedures address concerns related to appearance by enhancing or reshaping features of the face and body, using a range of treatments that include both non-surgical options and surgical interventions.

Pre-Prosthetic Surgery

Pre-prosthetic surgery addresses anatomical conditions in the mouth that may hinder the fit and function of dental prostheses, using surgical procedures to optimize the oral environment for comfortable, stable, and effective denture placement.

Sedation and Anaesthesia

Sedation and anaesthesia address patient comfort and anxiety during dental procedures by using medications that provide relaxation or pain control, ranging from mild sedation to general anaesthesia based on the needs of the patient and procedure.

About

Dr Sallies completed his undergraduate training at the University of the Western Cape. After gaining valuable experience in general practice, he joined the Department of Maxillofacial and Oral Surgery at Tygerberg Oral Health Centre, where he worked as a Dental Officer, Lecturer, and Clinical Supervisor in Maxillofacial and Oral Surgery.

During this time, he also obtained a Postgraduate Diploma in Maxillofacial and Oral Surgery.

Driven by a passion for surgical excellence, Dr Sallies pursued specialist training in Maxillofacial and Oral Surgery across Tygerberg Hospital, Groote Schuur Hospital, and the Red Cross War Memorial Children’s Hospitals. He completed his specialist program and was awarded a Master’s degree in Maxillofacial and Oral Surgery from the University of the Western Cape. He was subsequently admitted as a Fellow of the College of Maxillofacial and Oral Surgeons of South Africa..

Dr Sallies is committed to delivering high-quality surgical care with precision, compassion, and a patient-centred approach, aiming to achieve the best possible functional and aesthetic outcomes for every patient.

Health Professions Council of South Africa (HPCSA)

The HPCSA is a statutory body that regulates health professions in South Africa, ensuring practitioners meet standards for education, training, ethics, and professional conduct to protect the public and guide the professions

Colleges of Medicine of South Africa (CMSA)

The Colleges of Medicine of South Africa (CMSA) is the national examining body that oversees specialist medical and dental training and examinations across South Africa.

South African Society of Maxillofacial and Oral Surgeons (SASMFOS)

SASMFOS is a non-profit association representing maxillofacial and oral surgeons, supporting specialist skills, professional development, and advocacy for both practitioners and patient care in South Africa.
